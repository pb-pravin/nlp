Do you want to know something about children in Africa? What do they do for fun every day? Find out here. Education School is expensive for many African children. Lots of families can't afford school uniforms or exercise books even they don't have to pay for school. For those lucky enough to go to school, they have a lot to learn. Some take two language classes: English or French, and their first languages. There is also math, science, history, social studies and geography. Chores take up much of children's time after school. They have to get water and fire for the family. Also there's cleaning, washing and helping Mum with the meals. Daily fun It's not all work and no play. Sports are very popular. Children make their own footballs with plastic and play it in the countryside and the streets of old towns. There are also many football teams for teenagers in Africa. Internet It's really expensive to go on the Internet here. Surfing the Internet for 20 hours costs over 600 yuan. This is more than the average monthly pay per person. Some schools offer computer lessons but few students can enjoy computer fun at home. Egypt and South Africa are the top two users of the Internet in Africa. All the big cities in the two countries can get on the Internet.
