Chen Han, a teacher in Beijing No.15 Middle School, gives young students advice on how to deal with danger. If you are robbed. Keep calm. If you cannot cry for help or run away, give the robber your money. Try to remember what the robber looks like and tell the police later. If you are in a traffic accident . If a car hits you, you should remember the car number. If it is a bicycle, try to call your parents before you let the rider go. If it is raining hard and there is lightning . Don't stay in high places and stay away from trees. When there is a fire, get away as fast as you can. If there is a fire. Put wet things on your body and try to find an exit. Do not take the lift. If someone is drowning . If you can't swim, don't get into the river. Cry out for help. Remember that danger is never as far away as you think. Take care of youself at all times!
